## Tone Rules

### DO
- Use precise technical language
- Lead with outcomes and shipped work
- Back claims with project references or placeholders
- Be direct and confident without arrogance
- Use active voice ("Built," "Designed," "Shipped")

### DON'T
- Use buzzwords: "passionate," "motivated," "driven," "enthusiastic"
- Claim expertise without evidence
- Use vague superlatives: "amazing," "incredible," "world-class"
- Invent metrics or statistics
- Use "I love coding" or any variation
- Over-explain — trust the reader's intelligence
